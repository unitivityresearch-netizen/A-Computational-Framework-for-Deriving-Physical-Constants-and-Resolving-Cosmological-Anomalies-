## The Criterion

A framework Φ claiming κ* = 3.0 is validated **if and only if** it satisfies all four conditions simultaneously.

### Condition 1: Topological Quantization Lock
Φ must exhibit a super-attractive fixed point at an integer value κ* such that:
- f(κ*) = κ* (fixed point)
- f'(κ*) = 0 (super-attractive — zero derivative guarantees universality)
- D(κ) → ∞ for κ ≠ κ* (geometric damping destroys non-integer fields in ~10⁻⁴³ s)

**κ=3 framework:** f(κ) = ½(κ + 9/κ), f'(3) = 0. ✓ SATISFIES

### Condition 2: Dual Manifold Scaling (C/κ and κ/C Duality)
Φ must define two conjugate scaling limits forming a closed renormalisation loop:
- Condensation Map: κ/C ~ ℵ₁ (discrete-to-continuum)
- Exactness Map: C/κ ~ 1/ε (continuum-to-discrete)

**κ=3 framework:** κ/π = 3/π (discrete/continuum) and π/κ = π/3 (continuum/discrete). Loop closes. ✓ SATISFIES

### Condition 3: Independent Domain Isomorphism
Φ must predict κ* = 3 in **at least three independent empirical domains** via derivation, not post-hoc fitting.

**κ=3 framework:** κ=3 derived from hexagonal geometry, E₈ algebra, logistic map, and 12×12 matrix eigenspectrum — four independent derivations. ✓ SATISFIES

### Condition 4: Kernel Completeness
Φ must derive its particle/content spectrum from a minimal topological kernel. The Standard Model gauge structure and three fermion generations must emerge as the unique low-energy effective theory.

**κ=3 framework:** E₈ ⊃ E₆ × SU(3)_F forces 3 generations via the (27,3) representation. SU(3)_colour, SU(2)_L, U(1)_Y all emerge. ✓ SATISFIES

## Why Each Theory Fails

### String Theory
- **Condition 1 FAILS:** No unique fixed point. The landscape contains ~10⁵⁰⁰ vacua with no selection principle.
- **Condition 2 PARTIAL:** T-duality exists conceptually, but is not operational at all scales.
- **Condition 3 FAILS:** No cross-domain predictions outside high-energy particle physics.
- **Condition 4 FAILS:** Cannot uniquely predict the Standard Model gauge group or generation count.

### Loop Quantum Gravity
- **Condition 1 PARTIAL:** Discrete spin networks exist, but no unique integer fixed point emerges.
- **Condition 2 PARTIAL:** Background independence is dual in spirit, but no operational maps defined.
- **Condition 3 FAILS:** Only addresses quantum gravity. No biological, cosmological, or materials predictions.
- **Condition 4 FAILS:** Has not produced Standard Model particle content.

### ΛCDM Cosmology
- **Condition 1 FAILS:** Six or more free parameters (Ω_b, Ω_DM, H₀, n_s, A_s, τ) fitted to data, not derived.
- **Condition 2 FAILS:** Classical framework with no quantum-continuum duality.
- **Condition 3 FAILS:** Cosmology-only. No particle physics, biological, or materials predictions.
- **Condition 4 FAILS:** Assumes the Standard Model as input.

### Standard Model
- **Condition 1 FAILS:** 19 free parameters. No derivation of their values.
- **Condition 2 FAILS:** No duality between discrete and continuous descriptions.
- **Condition 3 FAILS:** Single domain (particle physics).
- **Condition 4 FAILS:** Generation count (3) is an input, not a prediction.

## Conclusion

**No existing fundamental theory satisfies all four conditions simultaneously.**

The Four-Fold Criterion is not a post-hoc rationalisation of the κ=3 framework's success. It is a statement of what physical completeness *requires*, derived from first principles of consistency between quantum field theory and general relativity.
